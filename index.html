Planets of Our Solar System

The solar system consists of the Sun and the objects that orbit it—including eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. Here's a quick look at the main planets:

Mercury

Closest to the Sun and the smallest planet. It has no atmosphere and extreme temperature variations.

Venus

Similar in size to Earth but with a thick, toxic atmosphere. It has surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

Our home planet, the only one known to support life. 70% of its surface is covered by water.

Mars

The "Red Planet" with the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system. A prime target for future exploration.

Jupiter

The largest planet, known for its Great Red Spot and dozens of moons, including Europa and Ganymede.

Saturn

Famous for its stunning ring system. It’s mostly made of hydrogen and has over 80 moons.

Uranus

An icy gas giant with a blue-green color due to methane. It rotates on its side!

Neptune

Farthest known planet. It has supersonic winds and a deep blue color due to methane in the atmosphere.
